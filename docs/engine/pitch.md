# Game Pitch

*This page is the descope razor. Re-read it at every milestone exit; any
feature that doesn't serve it defaults to a later ring or the cut list.*

## One line

An embodied co-op colony-sim: you live inside the town you run — assigning
tasks to NPCs you know by name, building production chains, defending against
factions — in a procedurally growing world where towns evolve, empires rise
and fall, death is permanent, choices have consequences, and everything is
moddable.

## The fantasy

You are not a cursor above a colony. You are a person in it — with a house,
a hammer, and neighbours who remember what you did. The tactical view is a
tool you raise to plan; the world you drop back into is the place you
actually live. Think Bellwright and Medieval Dynasty's embodiment, RimWorld's
consequence, Kenshi's lived-in factions — together, in co-op.

## Pillars

1. **NPCs are people, not units.** Names, personality, morality, trust,
   relationships — and permadeath. Losing Wren the smith is losing *Wren*.
2. **You are in the world.** Embodied third-person; the tactical view is a
   tool, not a place you live.
3. **The world moves without you.** Factions war, settle, and collapse
   whether or not you're watching.
4. **Session-moddable co-op.** A friend adds an enemy with a JSON file and
   twenty lines of Luau, hash-verified into the session.

## One session

You spend the morning in the tactical view laying out a smelter row, then
walk down to the gate because Aldric — Named, distrustful since you
underpaid him — reports wolves took a hunter overnight. You lead three Crew
out to clear the den yourself, get home at dusk with pelts and one wounded,
and find your co-op partner has finished the smithy: the ore→smelt→smith
chain is live and the first iron spear goes to Aldric. Trust ticks up. That
night the dungeon to the east — which you have been ignoring — sends its
first raid.

## Why co-op strangers care

Embodied colony management is a hot, underserved niche in co-op — and here
your group's private mods travel with the session. Your friend's cursed
shrine, your crop mod, one shared town, no server setup beyond "join."

## Open questions

Promotion triggers and rates; morality mechanics; player death penalty in a
permadeath-NPC world; tactical-order reach; era count and names; enemy
family → unique resource mapping. Tracked here, decided by the milestones
that need them.
